Cllr: log-LR cost

0.00 = very well calibrated, ≈1.00 = no information

How accurate and well-calibrated the feature's likelihood ratios are. A reference system that knows nothing scores ≈ 1. Lower means better callibration.

EER: equal error rate

0% = Good at separating samples, 50% cannot separate samples

The operating point where false-positive and false-negative rates meet. Lower scores means the algorithm is better at separating authentic from non-authentic samples.

Standard Deviation

small = repeatable, large = volatile

Spread of Cllr across cross-validation folds. Small deviation means the score is trustworthy; large deviation means it swings between runs.
